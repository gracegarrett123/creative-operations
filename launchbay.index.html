Role-Based Training · The Three Tiers

From Michaela call May 14 — prevent user overwhelm by training by role, not by feature
›
Michaela's recommendation: don't train everyone on everything. Train each person on the slice of LB they actually use. Three role tiers · separate training paths · much faster ramp.
Tier 1
Builders
Create & maintain templates, build new portals, configure automations
Who at Crain: Grace (today) · Natalie (deputy) · AdOps delegate (by Day 90) · Michaela (vendor)
Training: Deep platform fluency · template construction · Zapier integration · permissions admin
Tier 2
Daily Users
Operate within templates · run client portals · execute the 8-phase workflow
Who at Crain: Sophia · Brittany · Veronica · kathy · Shelly · Sammy backfill · Jordan
Training: Their portfolio + workflow only · client invite + content review focus · graduates to "Review content before design" steady state
Tier 3
Managers
Review, oversee, see dashboards · don't operate
Who at Crain: Kristin · Deana · Kristen Thornton · Sales leadership
Training: Read-only dashboard tour · how to ask "what's the LB count this week" · escalation paths
Why this is a big deal: The old plan trained 12 champions on the full platform. The role-based approach is faster (each tier ramps in their slice), more durable (people retain what they actually use), and matches the workload-shift framing (Daily Users do less, not more, over time).

Making Launchbay Stick · 4 Reinforcement Layers

How adoption becomes habit, not effort
›
Voluntary tool adoption usually plateaus at 30–40% without structural reinforcement. To hit 50%+ at Day 90 and stay there, four mechanisms layer on each other — each one alone is fragile; together they hold.
⚙️
Layer 1 · Process
Make LB the path of least resistance
  • LB project ID becomes a required field in Smartsheet IO intake — no ID, no kickoff
  • Status syncs auto from LB → SS so PMs don't update both places
  • Intake form replaces email for client asset collection
  • Client invite is the kickoff trigger, not an email thread
Owner: Grace · Smartsheet automation
👥
Layer 2 · People
Weekly visibility from leadership
  • Kristin asks brand leads weekly: "What's your team's LB portal count this week?"
  • Champion check-ins with Grace / Natalie every 2 weeks
  • Monthly Michaela syncs for ongoing CS / platform feedback
  • Trailblazers Slack stays active as peer-support channel
Owner: Kristin + Grace
📊
Layer 3 · Scorecard
Tie it to performance reviews
  • Q3/Q4 perf reviews include "% of digital articles run in LB"
  • Champion goal: 3 LB projects shipped without escalation = graduated
  • Deputy goal (Natalie): Cross-brand superuser by Day 90 (Aug 24)
  • AdOps goal: Solo-run ≥1 portal by Day 90
Owner: Kristin (formalize with HR)
💰
Layer 4 · Renewal Story
Make LB usage = sales advantage
  • Renewal narratives pulled from LB data, not memory or scattered emails
  • Client NPS / engagement visible per portal — sales talking point
  • "What happened on this campaign?" answered in one place
  • Recurring clients (MAHP, Wintrust, Solera) compound the data advantage
Owner: AdOps + Sales leadership

🔁 The 8-phase workflow every portal will follow›

This is the standardized workflow Comcast already runs. Every new portal will use this same shape, with brand-specific timing variants.

  1. Kickoff & Intake — call, scope, intake form, client outreach cadence
  2. Asset & Copy — V1 draft + assets received, strategist review
  3. Editorial — V1 → freelance → client → V2 → final approval
  4. Design Hub — Arc composition, AdOps places banners
  5. Preview & Approval — client signs off on composed article
  6. Distribution Setup — newsletter, right rail, native, social
  7. Launch — verify, go live, deliver live link
  8. Reporting — wrap report to client + LB record
